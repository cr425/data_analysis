Here is an initial view of the loan amounts based on the income bracket. From this scatter plot, we can see that:

  • Most loans are adminstered to those with an annual income less than $500,000
  • As the loan amount increases, the number of loans also decreases

Upon further exploration, one can determine the income bracket that requests the most amount of loans and unique characteristics about those individuals.



As expected, most of the loans are administered to individuals with an income of less than $125,000. Some noteable features are:

  • 85% of these loans are taken out by individuals with an income less than $75,000
    • $0 - $25000: 2598
    • $25000 - $50000: 13254
    • $50000 - $75000: 13002
    • $75000 - $100000: 6986
    • $100000 - $125000: 3305
    • $125000 - $150000: 1395
    • $150000 - $175000: 803
    • $175000 - $200000: 503


From the individuals whose annual income is less than $500,000, the grade distribution of those loans are as follows:

  • A: 23.94 %
  • B: 29.12 %
  • C: 20.53 %
  • D: 14.15 %
  • E: 7.97 %
  • F: 3.06 %
  • G: 1.19 %

The distribution appears to be right-skewed and it appears that most of the loans for this distribution fall into the B category which implies higher interest rates and fee structure.
